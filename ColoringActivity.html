Thorax: Chest Wall

The chest wall contains the rib cage that gives attachment to muscles of the head and neck, the shoulder girdle, the back, the abdomen and the diaphragm. The cage is formed of the sternum anteriorly, the thoracic vertebrae posteriorly and twelve pairs of ribs between. The chest cavity houses and protects the heart, lungs and great vessels, and cage movement facilitates respiration.
